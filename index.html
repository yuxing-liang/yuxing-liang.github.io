Progressive Choices in School Choice Problems
By: Yuxing Liang

When studying the college admission or school choice problem, it is common to assume that students' preferences are treated symmetrically. In reality, however, students are often constrained in their preferences asymmetrically due to demographic or academic reasons. In line with reality, this paper is the first one to examine serial mechanisms that use an asymmetric treatment of students in a college admission or school choice problem with strict and homogeneous priorities for schools. The strict homogeneous priorities may be based on centralized tests scores or some other criteria that are common to all schools. We call these serial mechanisms Progressive Choice (PC) mechanisms since they allow more choices for under- performing or low-priority students. The class of PC mechanisms offers the exibility of a wide range of mechanisms to choose from that can help to achieve more equitable matchings than under the usual symmetric treatment. The class of PC mechanisms bridge the Immediate Acceptance (IA) mechanism and the Serial Dictatorship (SD) mechanism, which are its two extreme members. All PC mechanisms are Pareto efficient. This paper also studies the relative manipulability of PC mechanisms and compares them based on justified envy.
