I am a Ph.D. candidate in Economics at Concordia University, supervised by Prof. Szilvia Pápai and Prof. Xintong Han. My research lies at the intersection of market design, matching theory, empirical mechanism design, and quantitative methods. I am particularly interested in how assignment mechanisms shape incentives, welfare, and preference revelation in education and related allocation environments.

My work combines theoretical mechanism design with empirical and computational methods. I use tools from matching theory, choice modelling, simulation, and applied data analysis, with programming experience in Python, MATLAB, Stata, and R.

Working Papers

Towards a Better Major Assignment: An Empirical Study with Students in China

with Xintong Han and Yan Zeng

This project studies major assignment in Chinese higher education using student preference data and mechanism-based counterfactual analysis. It evaluates how alternative assignment rules affect welfare, first-choice placement, distributional outcomes, and the incentives students face when submitting rank-order lists.

Progressive Choices in School Choice Problems

with Szilvia Pápai

This paper studies school choice mechanisms that permit asymmetric choice opportunities under strict homogeneous priorities. The analysis connects progressive choice mechanisms to the Immediate Acceptance and Serial Dictatorship mechanisms and examines efficiency, manipulability, and justified envy.

Bridging Theory and Evidence: How External Information Transforms Students' Application Decisions

Work in progress

This project investigates how information and application environments affect students' submitted preferences, assignment outcomes, and the empirical interpretation of preference-revelation behavior.

Projects & Data

Matching Simulation Framework with Homogeneous Priorities

MATLAB

This simulation framework studies assignment outcomes under mechanisms such as Deferred Acceptance and Serial Dictatorship. It can be used to enumerate feasible matching outcomes, compare Pareto efficiency, and examine manipulation incentives under different market configurations.

Choice Modelling and Assignment Analysis

Python, MATLAB, Stata, R

My empirical work uses discrete-choice models, assignment simulations, and counterfactual mechanism analysis to study how students form applications and how institutions allocate seats under capacity and priority constraints.
